Metabase: An Open-Source Business Intelligence Tool for AI & ML

  • Supervised Learning
  • By: Selvakumar Ulaganthan, PhD


Introduction

Effective presentation and interactive visualisation of outputs from various machine learning models are vital aspects of bringing Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning close to the actual businesses. In this respect, Business Intelligence (BI) tools play a significant role in bringing various AI & ML solutions to the hands of business experts. The typical work-flow of BI tools, at least in the context of AI & ML, includes the followings.

  • Bringing data from various sources (including the results of AI models)
  • Creating plots which answer various business questions
  • Creating dashboards which provide various business insights in terms of a collection of large number of plots
  • Deploying the application either on-premise or in cloud
    •

To this end, BI tools often come up with features such as capabilities to create various plots and dashboards, easy integration with various database systems, etc. Although it is subject to the problem (or the business requirements rather) at hand, I often weigh the BI tools based on the following features.

  • Ability to easily connect to various databases
  • Ability to create plots which are suitable to present both table data & time-series data
  • Ability to apply various filtering during the creation of dashboards
  • Easy deployment in cloud services such as AWS, Azure, Heroku or Google cloud
  • Open-source or a commercial tool
    •

I recently came across an interesting open-sourced BI tool called Metabase . As a reluctant user of Qlik (although Qlik comes up with many ETL features), I was quickly impressed by the simplicity of Metabase. In this article, I will demonstrate how we can very easily build an AI dashboard using Metabase, and deploy it in AWS.


Metabase in AWS

I used AWS Free-Tier account in order to deploy Metabase in AWS for free. Metabase works very well with a basic EC2 T3 instance (either type micro or small). EC2 in Free Tier comes up with 750 free hours per month which is good enough for my case. In order to deploy the application, you can refer to one of these links : Metabase easy deployment , Metabase custom deployment or Youtube video .

Plots and Dashboards in Metabase

Plots are referred to as Questions in Metabase. Access to plots can be obtained once a raw data (e.g., table from a prostgres db) is selected from one of the integrated DBs. Some of the plot types which are currently suported by Metabase include (refer here for more information):

  • Line, Bar, Combination of Line & Bar
  • Area, Row, Scatter
  • Pie, Funnel, Trend
  • Table, Map
